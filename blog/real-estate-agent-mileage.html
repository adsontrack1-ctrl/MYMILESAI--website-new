Real Estate Agent Mileage Deduction: Save Thousands on Every Showing

How realtors can track and deduct business miles for showings, open houses, inspections, and client meetings in 2026.

Real Estate March 18, 2026 8 min read

Real estate is a driving-intensive business. Between showings, open houses, listing appointments, inspections, and closings, the average agent logs 10,000 to 15,000 business miles per year. At the 2026 IRS rate of $0.725/mile, that's $7,250 to $10,875 in tax deductions — money you're leaving on the table if you don't track.

What Miles Are Deductible for Realtors

Fully deductible:

  • Driving to and from property showings
  • Open house drives
  • Listing presentations and buyer consultations
  • Home inspections and appraisals
  • Closings and title company visits
  • Driving between properties
  • Networking events, association meetings, continuing education classes
  • Supply runs for staging, signage, or marketing materials
  • Bank, post office, and print shop trips for business

Not deductible:

  • Your commute from home to the brokerage office (unless you have a qualifying home office)
  • Personal errands during the workday
  • Driving to lunch unless it's a client meeting

The Home Office Game-Changer

Many agents primarily work from home — taking calls, managing listings online, scheduling showings. If you use a dedicated space in your home regularly and exclusively for your real estate business, it may qualify as your principal place of business.

With a qualifying home office, every business trip from your home — including the drive to your brokerage office — becomes deductible from the first mile. This single change can add 2,000-3,000 deductible miles per year for most agents.

Other Tax Deductions for Agents

Beyond mileage, real estate agents can deduct:

  • MLS fees and association dues
  • Marketing costs (signs, flyers, online ads, professional photography)
  • Continuing education and licensing fees
  • Business portion of phone and internet
  • Home office expenses
  • E&O insurance and business insurance
  • Client gifts (up to $25 per client per year)
  • Staging supplies and lockbox fees

Why Agents Need Automatic Tracking

Real estate agents have one of the hardest jobs to track manually — multiple short trips per day, constantly changing destinations, and a schedule that varies every week. Manual logging typically results in 30-40% of miles going unrecorded.

A GPS mileage tracking app solves this by running in the background and logging every trip automatically. At the end of each day, you swipe to classify trips as business or personal. No manual entry, no forgotten trips, no lost deductions.

Canadian Agents

Canadian real estate agents follow CRA rules. The 2026 CRA rate is $0.73/km for the first 5,000 business kilometres. You'll need a CRA-compliant logbook showing date, destination, purpose, and kilometres for each trip. File vehicle expenses on Form T2125.
